Solid Copper Reference

Temperature Correction

          
Copper resistance rises as wire temperature rises and falls when wire temperature is colder than 68°F. The app adjusts the resistance-per-foot before calculating distance.

        

      


      

        

          
How to Measure

          
    
            
  1. De-energize the circuit. Verify no voltage is present before using resistance mode.
    2. 
            
  2. Isolate the cable. Disconnect both conductors from equipment, loads, controls, relays, panels, or electronics.
    3. 
            
  3. Confirm the short. Check that the two conductors show low resistance to each other.
    4. 
            
  4. Zero your leads. Short the meter leads together and use REL/Zero if your meter has it.
    5. 
            
  5. Measure across the pair. Put one probe on each conductor at the same end of the cable.
    6. 
            
  6. Enter the reading. Use the measured loop resistance in ohms.
    7. 
          

        

        

          
Accuracy Tips

          
    
            
  • Clean copper contact points improve low-ohm readings.
    • 
            
  • Very short cables are harder to locate because meter lead resistance matters more.
    • 
            
  • Splices, corrosion, wet insulation, and multiple faults can skew results.
    • 
            
  • Estimate actual wire temperature, not room temperature, when the cable is in an attic, conduit, sun, freezer, or outside wall.
    • 
          

        

      


      

        

          
Settings

About

          
Fault Locator Pro estimates distance to a short in solid copper two-conductor cable using loop resistance.

          
This is an estimate. Actual fault location can vary due to meter accuracy, wire tolerance, temperature, splices, corrosion, and multiple faults.
