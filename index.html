    How to Test with a Digital Multimeter
    
    
      
  1. De-energize first. Turn the circuit off and verify no voltage is present before switching to ohms.
    2. 
      
  2. Isolate the cable. Disconnect both conductors from panels, modules, relays, EOL resistors, NAC appliances, detectors, and any connected equipment.
    3. 
      
  3. Discharge stored voltage. Long cable runs and connected electronics can hold stray voltage. Confirm the meter reads near 0 V before measuring resistance.
    4. 
      
  4. Zero the meter leads. Touch the probes together and use REL/Zero if available.
    5. 
      
  5. Across-pair test. Measure between the two conductors at the same end of the cable. This is the normal distance-to-short mode.
    6. 
      
  6. Ground-fault test. Measure each conductor to building ground, conduit, shield, or drain wire.
    7. 
      
  7. Check both ends if possible. A clean single fault should roughly agree when measured from opposite ends.
    8. 
    

  


  

    Troubleshooting Faults
    
    
      
  • Dead short near test end: Very low ohms. Recheck meter lead zero and inspect the first boxes, junctions, and terminations.
    • 
      
  • Ground fault: Test each conductor to ground. Look for staples, damaged jacket, wet boxes, conduit rub-through, shield/drain contact, or devices touching grounded metal.
    • 
      
  • Bad device or module: If the fault disappears when devices are removed, reconnect one device at a time until the fault returns.
    • 
      
  • Stray voltage: If voltage exists on the pair, resistance readings will be unreliable.
    • 
      
  • Partial or resistive fault: Higher-than-expected resistance can point to corrosion, water, loose splices, damaged insulation, or a device still in circuit.
    • 
    

  


  

    Klein ET450 Tracer Tips
    
    
      
  1. For de-energized tracing, isolate the cable and connect the transmitter leads to the target conductors per the Klein manual.
    2. 
      
  2. Start with lower receiver sensitivity and increase only as needed.
    3. 
      
  3. Trace from the test end toward the expected fault distance calculated by the app.
    4. 
      
  4. If the signal suddenly drops or spreads, inspect nearby boxes, splices, devices, metal edges, and damaged cable points.
    5. 
      
  5. Use NCV only as a voltage-presence aid. Confirm with a meter before touching conductors.
    6.
