DMM Test Procedure
  1. Verify power is off. Never use ohms mode on an energized circuit.
  2. Disconnect the cable. Remove both conductors from panels, modules, relays, EOL resistors, notification appliances, detector bases, and field devices.
  3. Check for stray voltage. Measure AC and DC voltage first. If voltage is present, find the source before using resistance mode.
  4. Zero the meter leads. Short the probes together and use REL/Zero if available.
  5. Across-pair test. Measure conductor-to-conductor for a short or loop reading.
  6. Ground-fault test. Measure each conductor to ground, conduit, shield, drain, or grounded metal.
  7. Compare from both ends. If possible, test from the opposite end to confirm the suspected distance.
Fault Locating Strategy
  • Dead short: Low ohms across the pair. Inspect near calculated distance, junction boxes, device bases, crushed cable, and splices.
  • Ground fault: Low ohms from conductor to ground. Look for staples, metal box contact, conduit rub-through, wet locations, shield/drain contact, or damaged jacket.
  • Bad device: If fault disappears after disconnecting devices, reconnect one at a time until the fault returns.
  • Stray voltage: Resistance readings become unreliable. Locate backfed voltage, induced voltage, shared neutrals, or connected electronics.
  • Resistive fault: Higher-than-expected reading may indicate water, corrosion, loose splice, partial open, wrong wire type, or devices still connected.
Klein ET450 Tracer Tips
  1. Use the ET450 as a tracing aid after the fault distance gives you a target area.
  2. For de-energized tracing, isolate the cable and connect the transmitter to the target conductors.
  3. Start with lower receiver sensitivity, then increase only as needed.
  4. Trace toward the calculated distance and inspect boxes or splices near that point.
  5. A sudden signal change can indicate a splice, branch, cable damage, or transition into another path.
  6. Use NCV only as an alert. Confirm voltage with a meter before touching conductors.

Legal Disclaimer

This app provides an engineering estimate only. Actual fault location can be affected by inaccurate wire selection, temperature, corrosion, splices, devices still connected, parallel paths, ground leakage, wet cable, meter accuracy, and multiple faults.

Always follow electrical safety practices, de-energize circuits before resistance testing, verify with a meter, and comply with applicable codes, manufacturer instructions, and site procedures.
