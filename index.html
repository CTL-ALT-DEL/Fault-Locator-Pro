DMM field procedure
  1. Verify AC and DC voltage are absent before using resistance mode.
  2. Disconnect both conductors from panels, modules, devices, EOL resistors, and surge protectors.
  3. Zero the meter leads with REL/Zero if available.
  4. Measure conductor-to-conductor for a pair short or loop test.
  5. Measure each conductor to ground, conduit, shield, or drain wire for ground faults.
  6. Reverse-test from the far end when possible to confirm the suspected location.
Fire alarm troubleshooting
  • Dead short: inspect recently serviced devices, splices, crushed cable, staples, and NAC appliances.
  • Ground fault: check wet boxes, conduit edges, shields, drain wires, metal backboxes, and damaged jackets.
  • High resistance: look for corrosion, loose splices, water intrusion, or devices still connected.
  • SLC circuits: isolate sections and remove modules or suppressors before resistance testing.
  • NAC circuits: remove EOL resistors and notification appliances before distance testing.
Klein ET450 workflow
  1. Use this app first to estimate the target distance.
  2. Isolate the cable and connect the tracer transmitter to the target conductors.
  3. Start with low receiver sensitivity and increase only as needed.
  4. Trace toward the calculated distance and inspect nearby boxes and splices.
  5. Use NCV only as an alert and confirm voltage with a meter.

Legal disclaimer

This app provides estimates only. Actual fault location can be affected by wrong wire selection, temperature, corrosion, wet cable, connected devices, parallel paths, multiple faults, and meter accuracy.

Always de-energize circuits before resistance testing and follow applicable codes, manufacturer instructions, and site safety procedures.
