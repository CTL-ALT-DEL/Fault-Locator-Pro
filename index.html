Solid Copper Reference

Temperature
Copper resistance increases when the cable is hotter than 68°F and decreases when colder. This app adjusts the resistance-per-foot before estimating distance.
Temperature is still an estimate. Use the actual cable environment when possible: attic, conduit, rooftop, freezer, crawlspace, or outside wall.
Prep the Cable
  1. De-energize. Turn power off and verify no voltage is present.
  2. Isolate both conductors. Disconnect from controls, devices, panels, relays, loads, electronics, and parallel paths.
  3. Expose clean copper. Use clean contact points so the meter reading is stable.
  4. Confirm the short. The app assumes a short exists between the two conductors.
Meter Steps
  1. Set the digital multimeter to resistance / ohms Ω.
  2. Short the meter leads together and use REL / Zero if available.
  3. Place one probe on each conductor at the same end of the cable.
  4. Wait for the reading to settle.
  5. Enter the measured loop resistance into the app.
Common Problems
  • Very low readings may be mostly meter lead resistance.
  • Readings beyond the cable length may mean the gauge, length, temperature, or isolation is wrong.
  • Wet cable, corrosion, splices, partial shorts, and multiple faults can create misleading results.
Settings
Calibration adjusts the resistance table. Use only after comparing against a known cable length.
About
